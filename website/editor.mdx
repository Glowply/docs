The Glowply visual editor lets you build and update your website without writing a single line of code. Every change you make is reflected on your live site immediately — there is no separate "publish" step.

## Opening the editor

From your dashboard, click **Edit Website** to enter the visual editor. Your site loads in full preview mode with an editing toolbar on the side.

## Editing your site

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select an element">
    Click any text block, image, button, or section on the page. A blue outline appears around the selected element and an editing panel opens on the side.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit text">
    With a text element selected, click directly on the text to type your changes. You can change the wording, adjust the font size, and toggle bold or italic formatting from the toolbar that appears above the text.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Replace an image">
    Click an image to select it, then click **Replace Image** in the side panel. Upload a photo from your device or choose one from the built-in image library.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Move a block">
    Hover over a section until you see the drag handle (six dots) on its left edge. Click and hold the handle, then drag the block to its new position. Release to drop it in place.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a new block">
    Click the **+** button that appears between sections while hovering. Choose a block type from the panel — text, image, gallery, contact form, and more.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete a block">
    Select a block, then click the trash icon in the side panel or at the top-right corner of the selected element. Confirm the deletion when prompted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview your changes">
    Click the **Preview** button in the toolbar to see your site as visitors will see it, with no editing handles visible. Click **Edit** to return to editing mode.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Real-time preview

As you edit, the canvas updates instantly. You do not need to save or refresh — what you see in the editor is what visitors see on your live site.

<Tip>
  Use the device toggle in the toolbar to switch between desktop, tablet, and mobile views. Check all three before you finish editing to make sure your layout looks good on every screen size.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  To undo a change, press **Ctrl+Z** (Windows) or **Cmd+Z** (Mac). Most actions support multiple undo steps within the same editing session.
</Tip>
